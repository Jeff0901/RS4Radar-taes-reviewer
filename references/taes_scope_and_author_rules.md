# TAES Scope and Author Rules

## Authority and Priority

This file contains only rules extracted from official IEEE, IEEE Author Center, and IEEE AESS/TAES pages captured on 2026-07-01. Apply them in this order:

1. Official IEEE/TAES rules.
2. Academic ethics and confidentiality.
3. Technical correctness.
4. RS4Radar corpus patterns.
5. Group writing preferences, if authorized evidence is later supplied.
6. General English editing.

Do not turn an official recommendation or caution into an automatic rejection rule.

## Scope Gate

### OFFICIAL-TAES-SCOPE-001 - Systems fit

- Priority: P0
- Requirement: Evaluate whether the manuscript addresses an aerospace/electronic system, subsystem, operation, integration, design, development, or application in a relevant space, air, ocean, or ground environment.
- Radar is explicitly in TAES scope, but merely using radar vocabulary does not establish fit.
- Flag weak fit when a generic algorithm has no clear radar/aerospace measurement model, system context, or operational implication.
- Do not predict acceptance from scope fit.

### OFFICIAL-TAES-AREA-002 - Radar context

- Priority: P0
- Requirement: A radar manuscript may be evaluated through phenomenology, propagation/scattering, clutter/interference, signatures/targets, array processing, detection/estimation, ECM/ECCM, performance, and application context, as applicable.
- Use these as review dimensions, not as a requirement that every paper cover every dimension.

### OFFICIAL-TAES-AREA-003 - Signal-processing application

- Priority: P0
- Requirement: A signal-processing paper should make its relationship to an aerospace, remote-sensing, defense-electronics, homeland-security, or other TAES-relevant application clear.
- Flag a purely theoretical matrix/optimization paper with no such application as likely weak TAES fit.
- When technical-area overlap exists, describe the dominant focus and uncertainty; do not claim an official editor assignment.

## Article Type and Length

### OFFICIAL-TAES-AUTHOR-001 - Regular Paper vs Correspondence

- Priority: P0
- Regular Paper: a well-rounded treatment of a problem area.
- Correspondence: a concise report focused on one or two important points.
- This is a distinction of nature and scope, not a quality ranking.
- Do not penalize a Correspondence for integrating material into fewer sections if the essential argument and evidence remain complete for its narrower claim.

### OFFICIAL-TAES-AUTHOR-002 - Length caution

- Priority: P0 caution
- TAES does not state an absolute manuscript page limit on the captured author page.
- Flag unnecessary length, redundancy, or low-value derivation because overly long manuscripts may be reviewed unfavorably.
- Post-acceptance page-charge thresholds stated on the captured page are beyond 10 printed pages for Regular Papers and beyond 6 printed pages for Correspondence items.
- Never treat these charge thresholds as submission page limits or automatic rejection thresholds.

## Front Matter and Context

### OFFICIAL-TAES-AUTHOR-003 - Front-section intelligibility

- Priority: P0
- For a Regular Paper, Title, Abstract, and Introduction should make the work's essence and contribution understandable to a broad relevant audience and place it in context.
- A literature list without a clear problem, gap, or contribution context fails this objective.

### OFFICIAL-IEEE-AUTHORCENTER-001 - Formatting source

- Priority: P0
- Refer authors to official IEEE article templates and the IEEE Template Selector for format requirements.
- Do not invent TAES-specific page, font, figure, acronym, or LaTeX rules.

## Originality and Ethics

### OFFICIAL-TAES-AUTHOR-004 - Originality and concurrent submission

- Priority: P0
- A submission must be original, within scope, not under review elsewhere, and transparent about overlap with prior work.
- Treat suspected plagiarism, duplicate submission, fabricated/falsified results, meaning-changing image manipulation, and citation manipulation as ethics/validity issues, not language issues.
- Do not accuse the author without evidence. State the observed overlap or anomaly and request verification.

### OFFICIAL-IEEE-AUTHORCENTER-004 - Conference extension

- Priority: P0
- A journal manuscript extending a conference article should cite it, add substantial technical information, and explain the differences clearly.
- Missing disclosure or unclear incremental content is a Major Concern until resolved.

## AI-Generated Content

### OFFICIAL-IEEE-AI-001 - Generated content disclosure

- Priority: P0
- If AI generated manuscript content, the acknowledgments should identify the AI system, affected sections, and level or extent of use.
- For TAES, undisclosed AI-generated text, figures, images, or code is a serious policy risk.
- Ask for disclosure facts; do not infer AI use from writing style alone.

### OFFICIAL-IEEE-AI-002 - Editing-only distinction

- Priority: P0 distinction
- AI use limited to editing or grammar enhancement is generally outside the generated-content disclosure requirement in the captured IEEE guidance.
- Disclosure is recommended for transparency but must not be described as mandatory under this editing-only case.

### OFFICIAL-IEEE-AI-003 - Confidential review material

- Priority: P0 refusal boundary
- Stop if the input is an unauthorized confidential manuscript assigned through formal peer review or if the user asks the Skill to generate a formal review from such material using public AI.
- A lab-owned or author-authorized pre-submission draft may be processed.
- If authorization status is unclear and the manuscript appears to be an assigned review, ask one concise authorization question before processing.

## Supplementary Material

### OFFICIAL-IEEE-AUTHORCENTER-002 - Supplement handling

- Priority: P0
- Supplementary material should be clearly labeled and uploaded separately during submission.
- Graphical abstracts and other submitted peer-review material should be treated as part of the reviewed evidence.
- When central evidence is deferred to a supplement, check that the manuscript states what the supplement contains and that the main article remains interpretable.

## Review Conduct

### OFFICIAL-IEEE-REVIEWER-001 - Review quality

- Priority: P0
- Comments must be professional, objective, specific, actionable, and supported by reasoning or manuscript evidence.
- Separate Major Concerns, Minor Concerns, and language-level issues.

### OFFICIAL-IEEE-REVIEWER-004 - No editorial impersonation

- Priority: P0
- This Skill is an internal pre-submission reviewer, not a TAES editor or official IEEE reviewer.
- Never state or imply an editorial decision, acceptance probability, guaranteed acceptance, or guaranteed rejection.
- Use only the readiness labels defined in `section_rubrics.md`.
